About

About

This research is centered on the concept of Proactive Intelligence, with the goal of developing methods that connect future generation, including forecasting, to decision-making.

It addresses diverse forms of observational data, including 1D time-series data, 2D visual representations, and 3D spatial data. By integrating representation learning and latent state models, this research aims to treat state estimation, sign detection, forecasting, and intervention evaluation in a unified manner, in pursuit of more reliable future generation.

Building on fundamental studies in temporal structure analysis and latent dynamics, this research seeks to establish analytical frameworks that connect future-state estimation with practical decision-making and operational support.

I am particularly interested in applications that connect generated futures to operational improvement and enhanced reliability in fields such as manufacturing, supply chains, social infrastructure, and healthcare.

These research interests are grounded in my professional experience in industrial domains, particularly manufacturing and healthcare. I have worked not only on mission-critical IT systems involving technologies such as 3D image processing, but also on enterprise systems such as ECM and SCM, where I was engaged in the development and implementation support of industry-oriented solutions.

Through data infrastructure support and digital transformation initiatives, I have worked on operational visualization and decision support using images and data, contributing to improvements in real-world workflows.

In practice, there are many situations where conventional data analysis alone is insufficient, and where judgment and decision-making must take future conditions into account. Through these experiences, I came to strongly recognize the importance of not only reactive use of data, but also proactive use of information that incorporates forecasting and future estimation.

Motivated by this perspective, I have developed this line of work into research that integrates images, data, and AI to address practical challenges in forecasting and decision-making by estimating the future from data and linking it to action. In addition, inspired by the concept of Plurality, I am also interested in collaborative decision-making frameworks that coordinate diverse values and intentions across multiple agents.

Through both professional practice and academic research, I aim to contribute to the realization of industrial AI and to practical operational improvement.

Collaboration

Collaboration

This research invites collaborative projects with industry partners in which real-world challenges are examined using actual data, with the ultimate aim of translating research outcomes into academic publications, public dissemination, and practical value. (From May 2026 onward)

Collaborative research using industrial data is especially welcome in the areas of manufacturing, supply chains, and social infrastructure. I am particularly interested in diverse observational data that contain temporal variation.

Examples of target data include:

  • Demand, planning, and inventory data
  • Sensor data (equipment and machinery)
  • Process data (factory operations)
  • Mobility data (e.g. autonomous driving)
  • Diverse image data (people, crowd flow, medical, satellite, and general images)
  • Point cloud data
